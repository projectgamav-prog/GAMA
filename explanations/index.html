You have the right to perform your duty, but not to the fruits of your actions.

Key Insight

In this video we explore Krishna's teaching that fulfillment comes from sincere action rather than attachment to outcomes.

Explanation

Duty Without Attachment

In this verse, Krishna explains that human beings have control over their actions but not over the results. This means that while we have the power to perform our duties, we should not be attached to the fruits of our labor. Attachment to our actions leads to suffering, as it creates a dependency on success and immediate results, which are not always in our control.
